INNER CIRCLE INVEST MEMBER LOAN 
POLICY PROPOSAL 
As at Date: 09-03-2025  
 
1. Introduction 
The member loan project was initiated during the initial stages of group formation but was 
temporarily shelved for one to two years to allow the group to achieve financial stability. This 
proposal outlines the structure for three types of loans: 
1. Short-Term Loan 
2. Medium-Term Loan 
3. Long-Term Loan (to be considered in the future) 
 
Short-Term Loan 
A dedicated emergency loan fund of Kes 300,000 will be set aside for members wishing to take 
short-term loans. 
• Loan applications ranging from Kes 10,000 to Kes 100,000 
• Three (3) months repayment period 
• Fixed interest rate of 10% 
• Roll-over option available for up to two consecutive months, with a 10% surcharge per 
month on the outstanding balance 
• A three-day grace period can be extended before penalties is applied. 
 
Medium-Term Loan 
The group will offer a medium-term loan facility, capped at 75% of a borrowing member's 
contributions. The maximum amount is subject to the executive committee's discretion, 
considering available funds and planned expenditures. 
• Loan applications ranging from Kes 60,000 to Kes 300,000 
• Repayment periods: 6 months to 12 months 
• Outstanding balances may be converted into a short-term loan for one month 
• Loan repayment is deducted from contributions if necessary 
• Processing fees and levies will be calculated from the actual cost of processing and 
disbursement 
• Fixed interest rate of 12%, unaffected by market fluctuations 
Additionally, the group will consider adopting an internet banking system for the joint 
Cooperative Bank account to streamline approvals and facilitate efficient fund disbursements.

2. Loan Application and Disbursement Process 
To enhance efficiency, loan applications must be submitted via the group's official email 
address to the executive committee for review and approval. 
• A standardized Member Loan Request Form will be introduced, capturing the loan 
amount, purpose, and a commitment to repay. 
• The executive committee will evaluate applications based on contribution history, 
internal credit score, and financial standing. 
• The group treasurer will notify applicants of approval or rejection, with clear reasons 
provided for any denial. 
• Emergency/short-term loans will be reviewed and decided within 24 hours; other loans 
will be processed within 48 hours. 
• Upon approval, the treasurer will disburse the loan amount to the borrower. 
• Any processing fees incurred will be added to the borrower's principal repayment 
amount. 
Summary: 
• Members submit loan requests via email. 
• The executive committee reviews applications and makes decisions. 
• Borrowers receive approval/rejection notifications from the treasurer. 
• Emergency loans are prioritized. 
• Approved loans are disbursed promptly. 
• Processing fees are included in repayment amounts. 
 
 
3. Interest Calculation 
Interest rates are fixed for the duration of the loan and do not accrue over time. Interest is 
deducted first before principal repayment. 
 
Example:

10% Kes 50,000 Kes 5,000 Kes 55,000 Kes 18,333 
 
*Any processing and fund transfer fees will be borne by the borrower.

• Loan repayments must be made monthly, on a fixed date corresponding to the loan

• Mpesa transfer charges to be covered by the borrower. 
• Credit scoring will not be implemented; instead, the executive committee will assess 
loan applications based on available funds and outstanding dues. 
• Loan repayment receipts must be sent to the treasurer for reconciliation and 
accounting. 
• Repayment funds must be sent to the group's bank account. If combined with monthly 
contributions, members must provide clarification for accurate record-keeping. 
 
5. Loan Penalties 
Failure to adhere to the repayment schedule disrupts fund availability for other members. 
Consistent repayment ensures the growth of the loan kitty and allows for increased borrowing 
capacity in the future. 
• A 10% surcharge will be applied monthly on overdue amounts, up to a maximum of two 
months. 
• After the two-month period, a daily penalty of Kes 100 will be imposed for up to a 
maximum of another 2 months. 
• A three-day grace period is granted before penalties are enforced. 
• If repayment is still outstanding beyond the extended period, the full outstanding 
amount will be deducted from the member’s contribution. 
• Members failing to reconcile their contributions by year-end will undergo a review 
process, potentially leading to removal from the group. 
To ensure fairness and transparency, a formal dispute resolution process will be established, 
with the executive committee handling loan-related disputes. Repayment meetings may be 
scheduled as needed to resolve non-performing loans.

6. Managing the Loan Portfolio 
Effective loan portfolio management is essential. The executive committee will be responsible 
for monitoring issued loans, ensuring timely repayments, identifying potential risks, and 
generating reports. 
Critical loan portfolio information to be shared with members: 
• Loan payments/recovery status 
• Outstanding loan balances 
• Defaults 
• Interest earned 
 
Members are expected to review shared Financial reports and promptly raise any concerns with 
the treasurer or Executive committee. 
The group may engage an external auditor, subject to member approval, for annual audits to 
validate loan portfolio performance and ensure transparency.
