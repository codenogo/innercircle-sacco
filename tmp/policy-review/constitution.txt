

Official Group Constitution


Last Update: 25 th April 2025

PREAMBLE
This is a group of like-minded men who have a passion for growth in all aspects of life. The members of the group have agreed to come together to build an investment group as envisioned in the group vision and mission. 
ARTICLE 1: GROUP PROFILE
The name of the group shall be INNERCIRCLE INVEST.
Vision
To foster a community where each member contributes to and benefits from our mutual success.
Mission
Build a trustworthy network of empowered men that genuinely promotes economic growth and social well-being for all members.
Group core values
	•	Trustworthiness: Must act in the best interests of the group.
	•	Collaboration and teamwork: Must work together to complete a shared goal.
	•	Transparency and accountability: endeavour to build trust and openness.
	•	Inclusive and accessible: Intend to build comradeship and support.
	•	Shared goals: Must be focused on attainment of the group goals.
	•	Commitment: Must be willing to contribute to group success by putting in the time and effort required.
ARTICLE 2: GOALS & OBJECTIVES
The Group aims:
	•	To empower each group member to actively contribute to shared financial success by pulling our finances together for savings & investment.
	•	To empower & network group members to tap into valuable opportunities within our different fields.
	•	To create a community of men whom we can genuinely support each other socially and economically.
	•	To facilitate accessible loans within a group setting to foster financial growth and prosperity for all members at affordable interest rates.
	•	To empower each member to enjoy the financial fruits of our investment efforts, directly benefiting our members in the form of dividends.
ARTICLE 3: GROUP MEMBERSHIP
Requirements for a person to be a member shall be: -
	•	A member shall be deemed terminated in event of death, mental incapacity and bankruptcy.
	•	Membership of the group shall be limited to vetted individuals who have been recommended by a group member. He should be above 18 years and interested in helping the group achieve its aims, willing to abide by the rules of the group and able to pay all backdated contributions and a goodwill agreed upon by the members.
	•	Every member shall pay an annual subscription of Ksh 120,000 through a monthly deposit of Ksh 10,000 not later than the 10th of every month.
	•	Every member shall contribute Ksh 1000 monthly to be received no later than the 10th of every month as a welfare contribution to aid in group welfare activities as outlined in the group constitution.
	•	Missing deadlines for payment of membership and welfare contributions shall attract a daily penalty of 100 KES until the arrears are settled in full
	•	Any Member desiring to resign from the group shall submit his resignation to the Chairperson and his resignation shall take effect from the date of receipt by the Chairperson of such notice.
	•	Membership shall be men only and limited to a maximum of eleven members, subject to review during an AGM by the registered members.
	•	Every individual member shall have one vote at General Meetings.
	•	The membership of any member may be terminated for any valid reason as deemed by the Members, but the member has a right to be heard by the group members before a final decision is made. A decision to terminate a member must be voted for by at least two thirds of the members. Termination must be effected within 30 days of official communication to the member.
	•	Any recommended person shall be eligible for membership of the group and shall be subject to members voting and subsequent announcement & confirmation of acceptance during a next group meeting to this request, become a member upon making a payment to match the total determined equity of existing members plus a goodwill amount that will be determined at the time of vetting the proposed new member.
	•	Any Member who falls into arrears with his monthly subscription for 3 consecutive months (90 days) from the 10th day of the month shall automatically cease to be a member of the group and his name shall be struck off the register of the members. The executive may, however, at its discretion, table for reinstatement of such members on payment of the total amount of subscription, fees and penalties outstanding to the group during extra ordinary meetings for consideration and approval by the registered members. Such members must however place a written request for reinstatement to the chairman/group executive for review & approval. Their reinstatement will follow the due vetting process of a new member proposed including the requirement to pay a goodwill.
	•	Any member may be expelled if he fails to attend FOUR (4) consecutive group meetings being absent without apologies where a discussion in the group will be conducted & a warning notice to the member shall be given during the third meeting.
ARTICLE 4. GROUP OFFICIALS
The office bearers of the group shall be:
	•	Chairperson
	•	Vice Chairperson
	•	Secretary
	•	Treasurer
	•	Vice Treasurer
The Office Bearers shall form the Executive Committee of the group.
The officials shall hold the offices for a term of 2 years. Thereafter elections shall be conducted to determine the office bearers. The officer bearers are eligible for re-election with a maximum of two subsequent terms of 2 years each.
All office bearers shall hold office from the date of election and shall be eligible for re-election.
The remuneration of the executive committee is slated for review and discussion during the AGM.
An Office bearer may cease being an official of the group if.
	•	He resigns from the office,
	•	He is removed from the office as provided under the rules,
	•	He ceases being a member of the group,
	•	In case of death,
	•	Upon expiry of the service period as set out in these rules and/or in any other way
	•	Voted out of the office,
	•	Is unable for one reason or another to discharge the duties of the particular office.
	•	ARTICLE 5: DUTIES & RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE OFFICIALS
	•	Chairperson
	•	Unless prevented by illness or other sufficient cause shall, convene and preside over all meetings.
	•	Shall be the Group’s spokesperson.
	•	He shall provide general policy guidelines related to the affairs of the group as expressly provided in the constitution and may co-opt any person to advise the committee in any capacity.
	•	In his absence the Vice-Chairman will chair the meeting. In the absence of both, the committee shall appoint one of its members(s) present to chair the meeting.
	•	Any other duty as shall be assigned at the AGM.
	•	Vice Chairperson
	•	Shall perform all the duties of the Chairperson during the Chairman’s absence.
	•	Shall Perform such other duties as shall be assigned by the Chairperson or the AGM
	•	Secretary
	•	Take and keep minutes of meetings.
	•	Prepare the agenda for meetings of the Group in consultation with the Chairperson. 
	•	Maintain the membership list.
	•	Collect and circulate any relevant information within the Group.
	•	Keep all the groups records.
	•	To write correspondences for the group
	•	He shall also be the custodian of all Group Documents, passwords and credentials.
	•	Treasurer
	•	Supervise the financial affairs of the Group.
	•	Keep proper accounts that show all monies collected and paid out by the Group.
	•	Shall receive and disburse under the direction of the executive all the moneys belonging to the group.
	•	Shall prepare all financial reports of the group and present them to the members.
	•	Shall be a signatory in the bank account.
	•	Shall lodge all monies belonging to the association with the bank and obtain receipts for all such transactions.
	•	Shall have custody of all the association’s cheque books and cash books.
	•	He shall be responsible for all statutory annual returns.
	•	Vice Treasurer
	•	Shall perform all the duties of the Treasurer during the Treasurer’s absence.
	•	Shall Perform such other duties as shall be assigned by the Treasurer or the AGM

ARTICLE 7. GENERAL MEETINGS
	•	There shall be three classes of general meetings, to be held either physically or virtually.
	•	Ordinary Meetings - Once every three months
	•	Annual General Meetings
	•	Special General Meetings
	•	The Annual General Meeting shall be held not later than 20th December in each year.
	•	Notice in writing of such annual general meeting, accompanied by the annual statement of account. The agenda for the meeting shall be sent to all members not less than 7 days before the proposed date of the meeting.
	•	The Agenda of the Annual General Meeting Shall include but not limited to: -
	•	Reading & adoption of the minutes of the previous annual general meeting.
	•	Matters arising.
	•	Financial Reports. 
	•	Election of office bearers (if the term has expired)
	•	Such other matters as the Committee may decide or as to which notice shall have been given in writing by a member or members to the secretary at least four weeks before the date of the meeting.
	•	Receiving of the annual reports on the Group
	•	Any other business with the approval of the Chairperson.
	•	A special general meeting may be called for any specific purpose by the Chairperson. Notice in writing of such meeting shall be sent to all members not less than 7 days before the date thereof.
	•	A special general meeting may also be requisitioned for a specific purpose by order in writing to the Secretary. The notice for such meeting shall be as shown in Article 7(b) and no matter shall be discussed other than that stated in the requisition.
	•	The quorum for meeting shall not be less than 2/3rd of the registered Members.
ARTICLE 8. PROCEDURE AT MEETINGS
a. Meetings shall be dissolved within 30 minutes when members present do not meet the quorum established.
b. Office bearers must discuss the meeting agenda to be acquainted with the most important issues to be discussed.
c. At all meetings of the group, the Chairperson shall take the chair or in his absence, the vice chair or in the absence of both these officers, a member selected by the members present.
d. Resolutions and questions arising from the meetings of the members shall be decided by a simple majority by show of hands.
e. Voting shall be done by members in attendance. Absent members cannot cast a vote via a proxy.
f. The Chairperson shall have a second or casting vote in cases of equal votes.
ARTICLE 9: ATTENDANCE AND CONDUCT OF MEMBERS
a) Members are expected to attend all general meetings virtually or physically if possible. Any member who fails to attend any formally convened meetings shall pay a fine of Ksh 1000.(Penalty is regardless of whether the chairman was informed or not. Informing the chairman is still expected as a courtesy to the group and fellow members) A member who is late to a meeting for more than 30 minutes shall be fined Ksh 500 payable within 48hrs. A daily penalty of Ksh 100 on any unpaid fines applies till all the fines are cleared.
b) Any agenda shall be approved by a majority of a qualifying quorum in attendance.
c) All members are expected to conduct themselves in a respectful and dignified manner during all meetings.
d) Members are expected to contribute to all discussions in the meeting in a non-acrimonious manner.
e) Matters of the group are strictly confidential and should only be discussed in official group channels. Currently, these are, the InnerCircle Invest WhatsApp channel, the group email (investinnercircle@gmail.com) and virtual/ physical official group meetings.
f) Any misconduct will be subject to the disciplinary action, which will be agreed upon by two-thirds majority members in a qualifying quorum whose decision shall be final.
g) The Group may limit the number of apologies a member can give within a calendar year.
ARTICLE 10. WELFARE OF MEMBERS
	•	A welfare sub-committee will be formed to support the group in managing various welfare activities outlined in the constitution on a case-by-case basis. 
	•	The Welfare will offer all members Psychosocial support, bonding and to promote interpersonal relationship between the members of the Welfare including but not limited to: -
i. Bereavement
To help a member during times of bereavement to his family as per guidelines prescribed under Article 10 (iv)
ii. Sickness and Hospitalisation
A member who shall fall sick and is hospitalised shall be entitled to benefit from the Welfare Funds as per Article 10 (c)
iii. Social events – Wedding, Birth, College Graduation
The Welfare will only disburse funds for the above referenced social events only.
iv. Beneficiaries
The beneficiaries of the welfare funds/allocations shall include:
	•	Registered member
	•	Nuclear family members declared by the registered member. This shall constitute the spouse, children aged below 18 (eighteen) years of age and children between 18 (eighteen) and 21 (twenty-one) years with evidence that the child is in a learning institution.
	•	Declared parents of the registered members, both from the member and/or spouse. The parents shall be the biological mother and a father to the member and spouse.
The allocations shall be as follows subject to review in an AGM.
Hospitalisation benefits will only be applicable to ONE (1) admission per person (Nuclear family) per year. 
	•	Registered Member
	•	Upon Hospital admission, due to illness, for more than three nights: Cash of Ksh 20,000
	•	Upon death: Cash of Ksh. 50,000
	•	Upon wedding: Gift worth Ksh. 10,000
	•	Upon Birth of a Child: Ksh  10,000
2. Declared spouse
	•	Upon hospital admission, due to illness, for more than three nights: Cash of Ksh. 20,000
	•	Upon death: Cash of Ksh 30,000
3. Declared child
	•	Upon hospital admission, due to illness, for more than three nights: Cash of Ksh. 10,000
	•	Upon death: Cash of Ksh 40,000

4.) Declared parent.
	•	Upon death: Cash of Ksh 20,000

	•	Next of Kin - Each member shall nominate a next of kin in writing who may be contacted in case of any emergency.
	•	Corporate Social Responsibility - Various CSR and get together activities to be determined by the members of the Welfare depending on available resources at least once a year. Any other cause as agreed by a 2/3 majority of the members can be considered for corporate social responsibility.
N/B - There shall be a cooling off period of 1 year from the date of formation to allow the welfare stabilises and effectively accommodates the above. During the cooling off period, financial decisions shall be approved by a two-thirds majority in cognizant of the available funds.
	•	Members Get-together:
	•	It shall be lawful for the members to congregate, relate, network or any other way deal with each other without offending this constitution and the Welfare encourages such associations provided they are not intended to undermine or discuss Welfare issues or in any way conflict with the objectives of this Welfare.
	•	Any other activity that is connected and may be carried out together with the other objectives of the Welfare and in furtherance of the Welfare’s objective and any other activity connected herewith.
	•	Payment for services rendered to group/expenditure.
	•	As and when need arises and to facilitate the objectives of the group. The executive office will table a report of the payments made during the Annual General Meetings.
	•	Approval of expenditure on groups' money shall be voted with 2/3rd majority of registered Members.
	•	Members shall receive dividends by the second week of December every year which will be accrued from penalties and loan interest.
	•	Death of a member
In case of death of a contributor, the stated benefits, dividends, shares etc shall be paid to an appointed nominee or nominees of the contributor as declared. Each member should provide the nominees to receive the funds as per the group welfare in case of death or any other natural disaster.
ARTICLE 11: CESSATION OF MEMBERSHIP
A member will be considered as a non-member if he fails to submit his monthly contributions for 3 consecutive months. A member who intends to leave the group shall notify the Chairperson in writing with a notice of 30 days. Members departing from the group will be entitled to Ksh 100% of the principal amount plus the interest. A refund shall be paid within 90 days from the receipt of such notice, less any outstanding loans and liabilities attached to the Member. The refund will be given in 2 instalments: 1st at 50% and 2nd one at 50%. A member shall also forfeit a convenient fee of Ksh 10% of their entire total net worth within the group at any given time.
ARTICLE 12: COMPLAINTS
All complaints towards the group shall be submitted in writing to the Secretary for presentation and deliberations and shall be discussed in the next Executive Committee meeting and the entire membership shall be advised on the resolution of all the complaints.
ARTICLE 13. AMENDMENT OF THE RULES OF THE GROUP
The rules of the group shall not be altered or amended except at an annual general meeting (AGM) or at the Extra-Ordinary General meeting called for that purpose. Proposals for amending or introducing a new rule shall not be valid unless passed by two thirds of the members. Notice of any proposed alteration of rules shall be submitted in writing to the Secretary not less than fourteen (14) days before the notified date of annual General Meeting or Extra-Ordinary General Meeting.
Any amendments to the constitution of the group must be approved by at least two thirds (2/3) majority of registered members in favour at a general meeting.

ARTICLE 15: DISSOLUTION
The Group shall not be dissolved except by resolution passed at the Annual General Meeting of members by a vote of a two-thirds majority of the members present. The quorum at the meeting shall be at least three-quarters of the registered members of the group. After dissolution, the funds of the group shall be shared out to all members according to each member's contributions net of all the costs expenses and all other outgoings.
